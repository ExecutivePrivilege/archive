Letter from Herbert G. Klein to Sam J. Ervin Jr. (Sept. 21, 1971)

[Letter from Herbert G. Klein, Director of Communications for the Executive Branch, to Sam J. Ervin Jr., Chairman, Subcommittee on Constitutional Rights, Committee on the Judiciary, United States Senate (Sept. 21, 1971), reprinted in Freedom of the Press: Hearing before the Subcomm. on Constitutional Rights of the S. Comm. on the Judiciary, 92nd Cong. 1299 (1972).]

[PDF | Master List]

THE WHITE HOUSE,
Washington, D.C., September 21, 1971.

Hon. SAM J. ERVIN, JR.,
Chairman, Subcommittee on Constitutional Rights, Committee on the Judiciary, U.S. Senate, Washington, D.C.

DEAR MR. CHAIRMAN: In response to your letter of September 16, 1971, requesting me to appear before your Subcommittee on September 29, 1971, I wish to advise you that, as a member of the immediate staff of the President, I must respectfully decline the invitation to testify.

Sincerely,

HERBERT G. KLEIN,
Director of Communications for the Executive Branch.
